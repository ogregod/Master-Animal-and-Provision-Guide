## ADDITIONAL ONION VARIETIES

### Baldur's Gate Red Onions
**Price:** 3 cp | **Weight:** 1 lb | **Uses:** 3 | **Type:** Food

**What you're buying:** A mesh bag containing 3-4 medium onions with deep purple-red outer skin and white flesh with red rings.

**Unidentified appearance:** Mesh bag of round bulbs with dark red-purple papery skin.

**Storage:**
- Same as yellow onions - cool, dry, good air circulation
- Actually store slightly better than yellow onions
- Keep away from potatoes
- Shelf life: 2-4 months if stored properly

**Usage notes:**
- One onion = approximately 1.5 cups diced (one use = 0.5 cup)
- Milder and sweeter than yellow onions
- Excellent raw in salads and cold dishes
- Beautiful color contrast in cooked dishes

**Flavor profile:**
- Mild, sweet flavor when raw
- Less pungent than yellow onions
- Retains some bite even when cooked
- Color bleeds slightly when cooked

**What to look for when buying:**
- Deep red-purple outer skin
- Firm bulbs with tight, dry skins
- White flesh with distinct red rings when cut
- No soft spots or sprouting

**Regional notes:** Imported through Baldur's Gate from southern growing regions. More expensive than yellow onions but prized for their mild flavor and striking appearance. Popular in taverns that cater to refined tastes.

**What you're buying:** A mesh bag containing 2 very large, flat onions with pale yellow skin and exceptionally sweet flesh.

**Unidentified appearance:** Mesh bag containing unusually large, flattened onion bulbs.

**Special characteristics:**

**Culinary applications:**
- Perfect for caramelized onion dishes
- Excellent grilled or roasted whole
- Mild enough for raw applications
- Traditional in noble house cuisine

**What to look for when buying:**
- Large, flattened shape
- Pale, papery skin
- Heavy for size (high water content)
- No soft spots (spoils quickly)

**Regional notes:** Specialty crop grown in the fertile coastal regions near Waterdeep. Expensive due to difficult cultivation and short storage life. Seasonal availability - best in late summer/early fall.

### Trielta Hills Shallots
**Price:** 1 sp | **Weight:** 0.5 lbs | **Uses:** 8 | **Type:** Food

**What you're buying:** A small net bag containing 6-8 elongated bulbs with copper-colored skin, each splitting into 2-3 cloves.

**Unidentified appearance:** Small bag of elongated, copper-colored bulbs.

- Store like garlic - cool, dry, good air circulation
- Actually store better than onions
- Keep in mesh bags or braided

- Delicate, complex flavor - milder than onions
- Essential in fine cooking
- More expensive but concentrated flavor

- Complex, wine-like flavor
- Milder and more refined than onions
- Cooks quickly due to small size
- Essential in sauces and vinaigrettes

**Professional techniques:**
- Brunoise (very fine dice) is traditional cut
- Excellent for pan sauces and reductions
- Caramelize beautifully
- Key ingredient in many classical sauces

**What to look for when buying:**
- Firm, elongated bulbs
- Copper to rose-colored skin
- No sprouting or soft spots
- Should feel heavy for size

**Regional notes:** Grown in the same Trielta Hills region famous for garlic. Considered essential by professional cooks. More expensive than onions but a little goes a long way.

## GARLIC VARIETIES & AGED PRODUCTS

**Price:** 1 sp | **Weight:** 0.1 lbs | **Uses:** 6 | **Type:** Food

**Culinary applications:**
- Spread on bread like jam
- Added to sauces for depth
- Garnish for meat dishes
- Incorporated into marinades

**What to look for when buying:**
- Completely black color throughout
- Soft, sticky texture
- Sweet, complex aroma
- No remaining white flesh

**Regional notes:** Made by aging regular Trielta Hills garlic in controlled conditions for 3-4 weeks. Expensive specialty item. Popular with adventurous cooks and those seeking complex flavors.

## CONSISTENCY NOTES FOR ROOTS & TUBERS EXPANSION

**Pricing Adjustments:**
- Specialty onions: 3-4 cp (vs 2 cp for standard yellow)
- Aged/processed items: 2-5x base price (black garlic, etc.)
- Colored/specialty potatoes: 6-10 cp (vs 5 cp for standard brown)
- Seasonal specialties: Premium pricing due to limited availability

**Weight/Uses Calculations:**
- Maintained consistent serving sizes across varieties
- Larger specialty items (elephant garlic) = fewer pieces but same total weight of product per use
- Specialty items often yield fewer uses due to premium nature and careful portioning

**Storage Life Variations:**
- Fresh/delicate items: Shorter storage (1-2 months)
- Traditional varieties: Standard storage (2-4 months) 
- Aged items: Extended storage (6-12 months)
- Seasonal items: Very short availability windows

**Regional Integration:**
- Used established Sword Coast locations (Waterdeep, Daggerford, Trielta Hills, etc.)
- Connected varieties to logical growing regions
- Maintained trade route and import/export logic
