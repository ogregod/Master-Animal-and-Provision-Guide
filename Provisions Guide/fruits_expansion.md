## CITRUS VARIETIES

### Waterdeep Lemons
**Price:** 3 cp | **Weight:** 0.5 lbs | **Uses:** 4 | **Type:** Food

**What you're buying:** 2-3 medium yellow lemons with thick, oily skin and very sour flesh.

**Unidentified appearance:** Small yellow, oval fruits with bumpy skin.

**Storage:**
- Room temperature for 1 week
- Cool storage extends to 3-4 weeks
- High acid content resists spoilage
- Don't store with other fruits (causes early ripening)

**Usage notes:**
- One use = 1/2 to 1 lemon (depending on size and application)
- Extremely sour, used for acid rather than eating fresh
- Zest (skin) is as valuable as juice
- Essential for preserving and flavoring

**Culinary applications:**
- Juice for acid balance in dishes
- Zest for aromatic oils
- Preserving agent for other foods
- Essential for many sauces

**What to look for when buying:**
- Heavy for size (indicates juiciness)
- Bright yellow color
- Firm, unblemished skin
- Strong citrus aroma

**Regional notes:** Grown in heated glasshouses around Waterdeep or imported from southern regions. Expensive but essential for professional cooking. Year-round availability.

## CONSISTENCY NOTES FOR FRUITS EXPANSION

**Pricing Structure:**
- Local/regional fruits: 2-6 cp per useful quantity
- Specialty/seasonal berries: 1-5 sp per smaller quantities
- Dried fruits: 1-4 sp per preserved quantity (concentrated value)
- Exotic imports: 8-15 gp per fruit (luxury status, transport costs)

**Weight/Uses Calculations:**
- Fresh local fruits: 4-8 uses per pound (2-4 oz servings)
- Small berries: 3-8 uses per smaller quantities (2-3 oz servings)
- Dried fruits: 6-12 uses per smaller quantities (concentrated, 1-1.5 oz servings)
- Exotic fruits: 2-8 uses per fruit (variable sizes, luxury portioning)

**Storage Life Patterns:**
- Fresh berries: 1-7 days (very perishable)
- Citrus fruits: 2-4 weeks (good storage)
- Dried fruits: 6 months - 2 years (excellent preservation)
- Exotic fruits: 2-7 days (extremely perishable, transport challenges)

**Regional Integration:**
- Used established trade routes (Waterdeep imports, Moonshae specialties, etc.)
- Connected growing conditions to logical climates
- Maintained import/export economics
- Emphasized seasonal availability for local fruits

**Rarity Distribution:**
- Common: Local fruits and basic varieties
- Uncommon: Regional specialties and out-of-season items
- Rare: Specialty berries and dried luxury items
- Very Rare: Exotic imports requiring special trade connections
