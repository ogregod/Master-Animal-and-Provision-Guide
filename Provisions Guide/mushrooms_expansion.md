## ADDITIONAL CULTIVATED VARIETIES

### Oyster Mushrooms
**Price:** 4 cp | **Weight:** 0.5 lbs | **Uses:** 4 | **Type:** Food

**What you're buying:** Fan-shaped mushrooms with white to gray caps growing in clusters.

**Unidentified appearance:** Clusters of fan-shaped, pale mushrooms.

**Storage:**
- Delicate - use within 3-4 days
- Keep cool and dry
- Don't store in plastic (causes sliminess)
- Remove any slimy specimens immediately

**Usage notes:**
- One use = 2 oz mushrooms (about 1/2 cup sliced)
- Mild, slightly sweet flavor
- Tender texture
- Quick-cooking variety

**Cultivation advantages:**
- Easier to cultivate than many varieties
- Consistent availability
- Less expensive than wild mushrooms
- Good introduction mushroom for wary customers

**Cooking characteristics:**
- Cooks quickly - don't overcook
- Mild flavor takes on other seasonings
- Pleasant, meaty texture
- Good for beginners

**What to look for when buying:**
- Firm, dry caps
- No slimy or dark areas
- Fresh, earthy aroma
- Intact cluster structure

**Regional notes:** Cultivated in controlled environments around major cities. More reliable than wild mushrooms. Popular with taverns wanting consistent mushroom dishes without foraging risks.

**What to look for when buying:**

- Verify forager credentials

**Regional notes:** Wild-harvested by expert foragers. Expensive due to risk and expertise required. Popular with experienced mushroom eaters. Essential to buy only from trusted sources.

### Truffle Mushrooms
**Price:** 25 gp | **Weight:** 0.1 lbs | **Uses:** 4 | **Type:** Food

**What you're buying:** Small, irregularly shaped underground fungi with incredibly intense aroma.

**Unidentified appearance:** Small, dark, rough-textured, lumpy objects.

**⚠️ EXTREME LUXURY ITEM:**
- Most expensive mushroom available
- Requires trained animals to locate
- Seasonal and location-specific
- Often pre-ordered by wealthy clients

**Storage:**
- Very short shelf life - use within days
- Wrap in cloth, keep cool
- Aroma diminishes rapidly
- Handle minimally

**Usage notes:**
- One use = 0.5-1 oz truffle
- Intensely aromatic and flavorful
- Used sparingly as flavoring
- Shaved thin over finished dishes

**Preparation methods:**
- Typically shaved or grated over food
- Never cooked extensively (destroys flavor)
- Added at end of cooking
- A little goes a very long way

**What to look for when buying:**
- Intense, distinctive aroma
- Firm texture (not soft)
- Verify authenticity (many fakes)
- Recent harvest date

**Regional notes:** Wild-harvested from specific forest conditions using trained pigs or dogs. Extremely expensive luxury item. Usually special-ordered. Symbol of ultimate culinary sophistication.
