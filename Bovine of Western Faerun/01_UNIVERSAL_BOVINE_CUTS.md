# UNIVERSAL BOVINE ANATOMY & CUTS
## Essential Knowledge for All Cattle Processing

*Every bovine - from common Daggerford cattle to exotic magical variants - shares the same basic anatomy. Eight primal cuts, consistent skeletal structure, predictable muscle groups. Learn this once, apply it to every breed you encounter. The specifics vary, but the foundation remains constant.*

**— Thadius Del Irontet, Master Butcher**

---

## FOUNDATION INTEGRATION

From **Master Butcher's Guide § Animal Classification & Anatomy, Bovine Family:**

**Universal Bovine Yields (1,200 lb baseline):**
- **Retail Cuts:** 39% (468 lbs) - steaks, roasts, ground beef
- **Bones:** 12% (144 lbs) - stock bones, marrow
- **Fat Trim:** 8% (96 lbs) - tallow, rendering
- **Hide:** 7% (84 lbs) - leather
- **Organs:** 4% (48 lbs) - heart, liver, tongue, kidneys
- **Blood:** 3% (36 lbs) - if collected
- **Waste/Loss:** 27% (324 lbs) - intestinal contents, processing loss

**Processing Timeline:**
- Slaughter to hang: 2-4 hours
- Aging: 14-21 days at 34-36°F
- Primal breakdown: 2-3 hours
- Retail fabrication: 5-8 hours

## THE EIGHT PRIMAL CUTS

Primals are the major sections created during initial breakdown. Each contains multiple retail cuts.

### **PRIMAL PERCENTAGES & PRIMARY USES**

| Primal | % of Carcass | Tenderness | Primary Cuts | Cooking Method |
|--------|--------------|------------|--------------|----------------|
| **Chuck** | 20% | Tough | Roasts, Ground Beef, Stew | Braise, Slow Cook |
| **Rib** | 10% | Very Tender | Ribeye Steaks, Prime Rib | Grill, Roast |
| **Short Loin** | 8% | Supreme | T-Bone, Strip Steak, Tenderloin | Grill, Pan-Sear |
| **Sirloin** | 6% | Moderate | Sirloin Steaks, Tri-Tip | Grill, Roast |
| **Round** | 22% | Tough | Lean Roasts, Ground Beef | Braise, Grind |
| **Brisket** | 4% | Tough | Pot Roast, Smoking | Braise, Smoke |
| **Plate** | 8% | Moderate | Short Ribs, Ground Beef | Braise, Grind |
| **Flank** | 4% | Tough | Flank Steak | Grill (thin sliced) |

### **SHORT LOIN - BUTCHER'S CHOICE**
**From Short Loin Primal**

**IMPORTANT:** The short loin contains both the strip loin and tenderloin. You must choose how to process it:

#### **Option A - Bone-In Traditional:**
- **8-10 T-Bone/Porterhouse Steaks** (16-20 oz each, bone-in)
- **Count:** 8-10 steaks per animal
- **Price per Steak:** 5-6 sp each
- **Note:** Porterhouse (rear section) has larger tenderloin than T-bone (front section)

#### **Option B - Boneless Premium (Higher Total Value):**
- **12-16 Filet Mignon** (6-8 oz medallions)
- **18-24 Strip Steaks** (12 oz boneless)
- **Price:** 3 sp per filet, 3-4 sp per strip steak
- **Note:** Tenderloin and strip are separated and cut individually

**You cannot have both options - choose one when processing.**

## YIELD CALCULATIONS

### **Live Weight to Retail Conversion**

**Standard Formula:**
```
Live Weight (1,200 lbs)
× Dressing % (62%) = 744 lbs dressed
× Aging retention (96%) = 714 lbs aged
× Retail % (39%) = 278 lbs retail cuts

Simplified: 1,200 lbs live → 278 lbs retail (23% total yield)
```

**Retail Cut Distribution (278 lbs total):**
- Premium cuts: 50 lbs (18%)
- Choice cuts: 92 lbs (33%)
- Standard cuts: 52 lbs (19%)
- Utility cuts: 84 lbs (30%)
