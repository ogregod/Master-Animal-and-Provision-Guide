# DAGGERFORD VALLEY DAIRY CATTLE
## Traditional Heartlands Dairy Production

*The Daggerford Valley produces milk, cheese, and butter for the Western Heartlands. These cattle are hardier than urban dairy breeds, bred for grass-based production on family farms. Good milk producers that also provide quality beef when culled.*

**— Thadius Del Irontet, Master Butcher**

---

## QUICK STATS

**Primary Purpose:** Dairy Production (Milk, Cheese, Butter)

**Secondary Purpose:** Beef (when culled)

**Origin:** Daggerford River Valley

**Availability:** Common in Western Heartlands, available on Sword Coast

**Climate Adaptation:** Temperate, grass-based

**Typical Herd Size:** 15-80 head (family dairy farms)

**Market Position:** Traditional dairy, good beef when culled

---

## PHYSICAL CHARACTERISTICS

**Dairy Cows:** 1,000-1,300 lbs (larger than urban dairy)
**Cull Cows for Beef:** 1,000-1,200 lbs

**Appearance:**
- Red, brown, or fawn colored (traditional)
- Gentle expression, large eyes
- Good body condition (grass-fed)
- Strong legs (pasture grazing)

**Temperament:** Very docile, family-friendly

---

## MILK PRODUCTION

**Daily Yield:** 3-5 gallons per cow (grass-based, seasonal variation)

**Lactation:** 10 months producing

**Productive Years:** 5-10 years

**Milk Quality:** Very high butterfat (5-6%), excellent for cheese

**Annual Revenue per Cow:** 150-200 gallons milk + cheese/butter production

---

## BEEF PROCESSING (Cull Cows)

### **Yields (1,100 lb cull cow - better than urban dairy)**

```
Live Weight: 1,100 lbs
↓ (61% dressing)
Dressed: 671 lbs
↓ (14 days aging)
Aged: 651 lbs
↓ (38% retail - better than urban dairy)
Retail Cuts: 247 lbs

Total Yield: 22.5% live to retail
```

**Better than urban dairy due to grass-based feeding and better body condition.**

## PURCHASE PRICING

**Productive Dairy Cows:** 550-850 sp

**Better priced than urban dairy cull cows** - better body condition from grass feeding.
