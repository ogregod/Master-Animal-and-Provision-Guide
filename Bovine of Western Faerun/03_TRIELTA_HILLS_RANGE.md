# TRIELTA HILLS RANGE CATTLE
## Economical Grass-Fed Beef

*For customers wanting cheaper beef than Daggerford Common, Trielta Hills Range cattle deliver. Leaner, rangier, fully grass-fed without finishing. Good beef at budget prices.*

**— Thadius Del Irontet, Master Butcher**

---

## QUICK STATS

**Purpose:** Beef Production (Budget/Economy)
**Origin:** Trielta Hills grasslands
**Availability:** Common in Western Heartlands
**Market Position:** Economy beef (cheaper than Daggerford Common)
**Processing Standard:** 1,150 lb steer at 24-30 months (slower to market weight)

---

## PHYSICAL CHARACTERISTICS

**Size:** 1,100-1,300 lbs (rangier, less muscled)
**Appearance:** Variable colors, hardy build, efficient grazers
**Temperament:** Semi-wild, more nervous than domestic breeds

---

## YIELDS & PROCESSING

```
Live: 1,150 lbs
Dressed: 690 lbs (60% - leaner)
Aged (21 days): 669 lbs
Retail: 247 lbs (37% - lower than Daggerford Common)

Total Yield: 21.5% live to retail
```

### **Processing Time**

**Total Active Labor:** 13 hours average

**Timeline:**
- Initial processing: 2-3 hours (slaughter to hung)
- Aging: 21 days (grass-fed, needs full aging)
- Primal breakdown: 2-3 hours
- Retail fabrication: 6-7 hours (less meat volume than premium breeds)

**Labor Cost:** ~52 sp (13 hours × 4 sp/hour journeyman rate)

**See Master Butcher's Guide § Economic Mathematics, Labor Cost Calculations**

**Leaner, less marbling, lower yields - but cheaper purchase price.**

## PURCHASE PRICING

**Live:** 2.8-3.2 sp/lb (vs. 3.5 for Daggerford Common)
**1,150 lb steer:** 3,200-3,700 sp (320-370 gp)

**CHEAPER to buy, LOWER yields, but still profitable for budget market.**
