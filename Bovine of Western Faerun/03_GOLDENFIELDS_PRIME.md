# GOLDENFIELDS PRIME BEEF CATTLE
## Grain-Finished Premium Beef

*Goldenfields has abundant grain. Some cattle get grain-finished for 90-120 days before processing, creating marbled premium beef that commands luxury prices. This is what wealthy customers expect.*

**— Thadius Del Irontet, Master Butcher**

---

## QUICK STATS

**Purpose:** Premium Beef Production
**Origin:** Goldenfields (grain-finished)
**Market Position:** Premium/Luxury (below Noble, above Common)
**Processing:** 1,300 lb steer at 24 months + 90-120 days grain finishing

---

## PHYSICAL CHARACTERISTICS

**Size:** 1,250-1,400 lbs (heavier from grain)
**Appearance:** Excellent muscling, visible fat cover, blocky build
**Condition:** Prime to Choice grade typical

---

## YIELDS & PROCESSING

```
Live: 1,300 lbs (heavier from grain)
Dressed: 845 lbs (65% - more fat)
Aged (21-28 days): 813 lbs
Retail: 325 lbs (40% - HIGHER yield, more marbling)

Total Yield: 25% live to retail (vs. 23% for grass-fed)
```

### **Processing Time**

**Total Active Labor:** 16 hours average

**Timeline:**
- Initial processing: 3 hours (slaughter to hung - heavier animal)
- Aging: 21-28 days (longer for optimal marbling development)
- Primal breakdown: 3 hours (more careful handling of premium quality)
- Retail fabrication: 8-10 hours (more meat volume, premium cuts)

**Labor Cost:** ~64 sp (16 hours × 4 sp/hour journeyman rate)

**Note:** Some butchers charge master rate (6 sp/hour = 96 sp) for premium grain-finished cattle due to careful handling requirements.

**See Master Butcher's Guide § Economic Mathematics, Labor Cost Calculations**

**Grain finishing increases marbling and overall yields.**
