# DAGGERFORD BROWN BEAR
## *Ursus arctos daggerfordensis* - The Premium Fat Producer

**ORIGIN:** Settled forests near Daggerford, Waterdeep outskirts
**PRIMARY ROLE:** Premium bear grease production, larger meat yields
**MARKET POSITION:** Urban demand for quality fat, controlled population
**PROCESSING DIFFICULTY:** Moderate-Hard (larger size, +4 hours vs black bear)
**UNIQUE FEATURE:** Highest bear fat yield (10% vs 8% standard)

## BREED OVERVIEW & IMPORTANCE

The Daggerford brown bear is the **bear grease specialist**. While black bears are the baseline for general processing, brown bears are what you hunt when you specifically want fat. 500 pounds live weight, 50 pounds of fat trim, 42 pounds of rendered bear grease worth 210 silver pieces. That's not meat hunting - that's **fat hunting**.

**Why This Breed Matters:**
- **Premium Fat Yield:** 10% body fat (vs 8% black bear) = 25% more grease per bear
- **Larger Size:** 67% heavier than black bear (500 vs 300 lbs) = more product overall
- **Urban Market Demand:** Waterdeep, Neverwinter bulk buyers always need bear grease
- **Controlled Population:** Near settlements, licensed hunting only (sustainable management)
- **Economic Specialist:** Fat value often exceeds meat value 2:1 (vs 1:1.4 for black bear)

**Market Position:**
- **Urban centers:** Primary bear grease source (Waterdeep, Neverwinter)
- **Bulk buyers:** Leatherworkers, soap makers, apothecaries (commercial quantities)
- **Premium product:** Higher quality fat (acorn/farm crop diet = better flavor)

**Processing Reality Check:**
- **Larger = More Labor:** +4 hours processing vs black bear (16 vs 12 hours)
- **Higher Investment:** Licensed hunting (15-25 sp), larger

 equipment needs
- **Better Profit:** 60-70 gp total vs 36 gp black bear (+80-100% revenue)

**Professional Perspective:**
*"Brown bears are for serious commercial hunters. You're not feeding your family with this - you're running a business. 500 pounds of bear, 40-50 pounds of grease, selling to Waterdeep bulk buyers. Know your market before you hunt."*

- **Habitat:** Ardeep Forest edges, wealthy estates

**Ardeep Forest (Settled Edges):**
- **Population:** Uncommon (30-50 individuals)
- **Access:** 1-2 days from Waterdeep or Daggerford
- **Hunting:** Licensed (10-20 sp depending on location)
- **Habitat:** Forest-farmland transition zones

### Why Settled Areas?

**Diet = Fat:**
- **Farm crops:** Corn, wheat, orchards (high calorie, more fat deposition)
- **Human refuse:** Food waste (rich diet = more fat)
- **Natural forage:** Acorns, berries (supplement)

**Result:** Higher fat percentage than wild forest black bears

**Trade-Off:** Human conflict (crop raiding, property damage) = controlled population

### Market Access

**Best Markets:**
1. **Waterdeep:** 0.5-1 day transport (bulk buyers, best prices for grease)
2. **Daggerford:** Local (immediate sale, lower prices but zero transport)
3. **Neverwinter:** 2-3 days (good prices, moderate transport cost)

**Transport Costs:**
- **Fresh meat** (500 lbs carcass requires wagon):
  - To Waterdeep: 15-25 sp (wagon rental, 1 day)
  - To Daggerford: 5-10 sp (cart, 0.5 day)

- **Rendered grease** (50 lbs in crocks):
  - Any local market: 5-10 sp (manageable weight)
  - Long distance: 2-3 sp per 100 miles

## PROCESSING SPECIFICATIONS

### Yield Data (500 lb Brown Bear - Standard)

**Live to Dressed (Field Dressing):**
```
Live Weight: 500 lbs
× 75% Dressing = 375 lbs dressed carcass

Removed in Field:
- Hide/pelt: 55 lbs (11%)
- Head: 25 lbs (5%)
- Feet: 12 lbs (2.4%)
- Entrails: 33 lbs (6.6%)
Total removed: 125 lbs (25%)
```

**Dressed to Retail (Butcher Shop):**
```
Dressed Weight: 375 lbs
× 48% Retail = 180 lbs retail cuts
(Slightly lower % than black bear due to higher fat content)

Breakdown:
- Retail cuts: 180 lbs (48% of dressed)
- Bones: 70 lbs (18.7% of dressed)
- Fat trim: 50 lbs (13.3% of dressed) **+67% vs black bear**

```

**Live to Retail (Complete):**
```
500 lbs live → 180 lbs retail = 36% total yield

(Slightly lower than black bear 37% due to more fat, less meat)
```

### **When to Hunt Brown Bear vs Black Bear**

**Choose Brown Bear When:**
- You can obtain a license (Daggerford, Waterdeep permits available)
- You have 3-4 days to process (longer timeline required)
- You have bulk buyer contacts (moving 42 lbs grease requires commercial buyer)

- No license available (open season areas)
- Limited time (1-2 days hunt+process)

- **Goal: Reliable profit, lower investment**
