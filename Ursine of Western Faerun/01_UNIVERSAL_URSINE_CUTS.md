# UNIVERSAL URSINE ANATOMY & CUTS
## Essential Knowledge for All Bear Processing

*Every bear - from common Sword Coast black bears to massive Northern grizzlies - shares the same basic anatomy. Eight primal cuts, consistent skeletal structure, predictable muscle groups. Learn this once, apply it to every species you encounter. The specifics vary (a grizzly has more meat, a sun bear has less fat), but the foundation remains constant.*

**— Thadius Del Irontet, Master Butcher**

## FOUNDATION INTEGRATION

From **Master Butcher's Guide § Animal Classification & Anatomy, Ursine Family:**

**Universal Ursine Yields (300 lb baseline black bear):**
- **Retail Cuts:** 37% (110 lbs) - steaks, roasts, ground bear, specialty cuts
- **Bones:** 15% (45 lbs) - stock bones, soup bones
- **Fat Trim:** 8-10% (24-30 lbs) - for rendering to bear grease
- **Hide/Pelt:** 10-12% (30-36 lbs) - leather, fur, trophy
- **Organs:** 4% (12 lbs) - heart, liver, kidneys
- **Alchemical:** <1% (gallbladder with bile)
- **Waste/Loss:** 25-27% (75-81 lbs) - intestinal contents, processing loss, blood

**Processing Timeline:**
- Field dressing: 2-3 hours (wilderness conditions)
- Hanging/cooling: 12-24 hours (not aged like beef)
- Primal breakdown: 3-4 hours
- Retail fabrication: 6-10 hours
- **Total: 23-41 hours** (wilderness to retail ready)

**Bear vs Cattle vs Pork Comparison:**
| Animal | Dressing % | Retail % | Aging | Fat Product | Primary Use |
|--------|-----------|----------|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Bear** | 75% | 37% | None | Bear grease (premium) | Wild game, fat rendering |
| **Cattle** | 62% | 23% | 14-21 days | Tallow (utility) | Primary meat, leather |
| **Pork** | 72% | 38.8% | None | Lard (premium) | Versatile meat, fat |

**Key Insight:** Bears yield like pork (high %, no aging, premium fat) but behave like wild game (seasonal variation, field dressing, diet impacts flavor).

## YIELD CALCULATIONS

### **Live Weight to Retail Conversion**

**Standard Formula (300 lb Black Bear - Baseline):**
```
Live Weight (300 lbs)
× Dressing % (75%) = 225 lbs dressed carcass
× Retail % (49% of dressed) = 110 lbs retail cuts

Simplified: 300 lbs live → 110 lbs retail (37% total yield)
```

**Why Higher Than Beef (23%)?**
- No aging required (processed immediately after cooling)
- Higher fat content (rendered separately as valuable product)
- Less waste (no rumen, simpler digestive system than cattle)
- Similar to pork (38.8% retail yield)

**Retail Cut Distribution (110 lbs total from 300 lb bear):**
- **Premium cuts:** 20 lbs (18%) - Backstrap/loin, rib roasts/steaks
- **Choice cuts:** 35 lbs (32%) - Shoulder roasts, ham roasts/steaks, ribs
- **Standard cuts:** 30 lbs (27%) - Ground bear, stew meat
- **Specialty/Utility:** 25 lbs (23%) - Bear bacon, paw meat, soup bones, organs

## PREMIUM CUTS (20 lbs, 18% of retail)

### **BACKSTRAP / LOIN - BUTCHER'S CHOICE**
**From Loin Primal (8 lbs total)**

**IMPORTANT:** The backstrap is the tenderest, most valuable cut. You must choose how to process it:

#### **Option A - Whole Backstrap (Special Order, Premium Customers):**
- **2 whole backstraps** @ 4 lbs each (one per side of spine)
- **Price per loin:** 4-5 sp/lb × 4 lbs = 16-20 sp each
- **Total value:** 32-40 sp
- **Best for:** Wealthy customers, special roasts, show-piece meals

#### **Option B - Backstrap Steaks (Individual Portions, Highest Total Value):**
- **10-12 backstrap steaks** @ 10-12 oz each
- **Price per steak:** 3-4 sp each
- **Total value:** 30-48 sp
- **Best for:** Individual sales, tavern menu, maximum profit
- **Note:** Steaks = 10-20% premium over equivalent weight in roast form

**You cannot have both options - choose one when processing.**

**Cutting Notes:**
- Backstrap runs along spine from shoulder to hip
- Remove whole before cutting (easier to work with)
- For steaks: Cut perpendicular to muscle grain, 1-1.5 inches thick
- Trim silver skin (tough connective tissue on surface)

---

### **RIBS - BUTCHER'S CHOICE**
**From Rib Primal (12 lbs total)**

#### **Option A - Rib Roasts (Family Size, Traditional):**
- **3-4 rib roasts** @ 3-4 lbs each
- **Price per roast:** 3 sp/lb × 3.5 lbs = 10.5 sp each (call it 10-11 sp)
- **Total value:** 30-44 sp
- **Best for:** Family meals, slow roasting, bone-in flavor

#### **Option B - Rib Steaks/Chops (Individual Portions, Versatile):**
- **8-10 rib steaks/chops** @ 12-16 oz each (bone-in)
- **Price per steak:** 2.5-3 sp each
- **Total value:** 20-30 sp
- **Best for:** Individual sales, grilling, faster cooking
- **Note:** Bone-in ribeye-style cuts, flavorful from marbling

**You cannot have both options - choose one when processing.**

**Cutting Notes:**
- Bear ribs similar to pork ribs (smaller than beef, meatier than pork)
- For roasts: Leave 3-4 ribs together, frenched or not
- For steaks: Cut between each rib bone, 1-1.5 inches thick
- Can remove bones entirely for boneless rib steaks (reduce weight to 10-12 oz)

## CHOICE CUTS (35 lbs, 32% of retail)

### **SHOULDER ROASTS**
**From Shoulder Primal (15 lbs total)**

**Standard Breakdown:**
- **3-4 shoulder roasts** @ 3-5 lbs each
- **Price per roast:** 1.5 sp/lb × 4 lbs = 6 sp each
- **Total value:** 18-24 sp

**Alternative Breakdown (Budget/Ground Needs):**
- **2 shoulder roasts** @ 5 lbs each (10 lbs)
- **5 lbs cubed for stew meat** (added to standard cuts section)
- **Use when:** Need more ground/stew, less demand for roasts

**Cutting Notes:**
- Shoulder has tough connective tissue (requires slow cooking)
- Cut across muscle groups (not with grain) for roasts
- Ideal for smoking (fat content, connective tissue breaks down)

---

### **HAM / HINDQUARTER - BUTCHER'S CHOICE**
**From Ham Primal (12 lbs total)**

#### **Option A - Ham Roasts (Traditional, Family Size):**
- **2-3 ham roasts** @ 4-6 lbs each
- **Price per roast:** 2 sp/lb × 5 lbs = 10 sp each
- **Total value:** 20-30 sp
- **Best for:** Traditional roasting, smoking (whole smoked ham)

#### **Option B - Ham Steaks (Individual Portions, Versatile):**
- **12-16 ham steaks** @ 12-16 oz each
- **Price per steak:** 1.8-2 sp each
- **Total value:** 21.6-32 sp
- **Best for:** Individual sales, grilling, faster cooking, variety

**You cannot have both options - choose one when processing.**

**Cutting Notes:**
- Ham is leaner than shoulder but more tender
- For roasts: Follow muscle groups (top round, bottom round, sirloin tip)
- For steaks: Cut perpendicular to bone (if bone-in) or across muscle grain
- Can be bone-in or boneless (boneless = easier portioning)

- **Price:** 2 sp/lb (6-8 sp per rack)
- **Total value:** 12-24 sp

**Cutting Notes:**
- These are the spare ribs/belly ribs (different from rib primal)

## STANDARD CUTS (30 lbs, 27% of retail)

### **GROUND BEAR**
**From Trim, Neck, Forelimb, Tougher Cuts**

**Standard Production:**
- **20 lbs ground bear** packaged in 1 lb portions
- **Price:** 1.5 sp per lb
- **Total value:** 30 sp
- **Fat content:** 15-20% (leaner than pork, fattier than venison)

**Uses:**
- Bear burgers (gamey flavor, hearty)
- Bear meatballs (Italian-style, with pork fat added)
- Bear chili (traditional frontier dish)
- Sausage base (mixed with pork fat, spices)

**Notes:**
- Wild game flavor (stronger than beef, milder than venison)
- Must cook thoroughly (trichinosis risk - internal temp 160°F minimum)
- Blends well with pork (50/50 mix reduces gaminess)

---

### **STEW MEAT**
**From Neck, Shank, Trim**

**Standard Production:**
- **10 lbs stew meat** cubed (1-inch pieces), packaged in 1 lb portions
- **Price:** 1.2 sp per lb
- **Total value:** 12 sp

**Uses:**
- Bear stew (slow-cooked, root vegetables)
- Bear pot roast (braised with onions, carrots)
- Bear chili (cubed instead of ground)

**Notes:**
- Requires 2-3 hours slow cooking (connective tissue breakdown)
- Excellent for smoking then stewing (double flavor)

## SPECIALTY CUTS (6 lbs, 5% of retail)

### **BEAR BACON**
**From Belly Primal (4 lbs cured weight from ~6 lbs fresh)**

**Production:**
- **4 lbs bear bacon** (cured, smoked belly)
- **Price:** 3-4 sp per lb
- **Total value:** 12-16 sp

**Process:**
1. Remove belly section (fattier than ham/shoulder)
2. Cure with salt, sugar, Prague Powder #1 (7-10 days)
3. Cold smoke 6-10 hours (apple or hickory wood)
4. Slice thin or sell as slab

**Notes:**
- Similar to pork bacon but gamier flavor
- Must cure properly (trichinosis risk)
- Frontier delicacy (combines meat preservation + unique flavor)

**Alternative Use:** If not making bacon, belly goes to ground/rendering fat

---

### **BEAR PAWS (SPECIALTY CULINARY)**
**From All Four Paws (2 lbs meat from 4 paws)**

**Production:**
- **4 bear paws** (specialty item, cultural/culinary)
- **Price:** 2-3 sp per paw
- **Total value:** 8-12 sp

**Uses:**
- **Braised bear paw:** Traditional delicacy (gelatinous, rich)
- **Bear paw stock:** Extremely gelatinous (high collagen)
- **Cultural significance:** Some regions prize this cut

**Processing:**
- Remove fur carefully (skinning)
- Separate paw at wrist/ankle joint
- Braise 4-6 hours (tough connective tissue, but yields gelatin)

**Market:**
- Specialty restaurants (exotic menu item)
- Cultural communities (traditional recipes)
- Adventurer curiosity (unique experience)

**Note:** Some regions/cultures avoid bear paws (resemblance to human hands = taboo). Know your market before processing.

## BYPRODUCTS (NOT COUNTED IN RETAIL CUTS)

### **BEAR FAT / BEAR GREASE**
**From Fat Trim Throughout Carcass**

**Yield (300 lb Black Bear):**
- **30 lbs fat trim** (10% of live weight - fall condition)
- **25 lbs rendered bear grease** (83% yield after rendering)

**Value:**
- Fresh fat: 1-2 sp/lb (30-60 sp total)
- Rendered grease: 4-6 sp/lb (100-150 sp total)
- **Value increase: +100-150% from rendering**

**Quality Grades:**
- **Leaf fat** (around organs): 6-8 sp/lb (whitest, mildest, best for baking)
- **Back fat** (dorsal layer): 4-6 sp/lb (standard cooking grease)
- **Trim fat** (mixed sources): 3-4 sp/lb (lower quality, soap/candles)

**Rendering Process:**
1. Collect all fat (back fat, belly fat, organ fat)
2. Cube into 1-inch pieces
3. **Wet rendering:** Simmer in water 6-8 hours at 180-200°F (easier, less skill)
4. **Dry rendering:** Slow heat in pot, no water (higher quality, more skill)
5. Strain through cheesecloth (remove cracklings)
6. Store in ceramic crocks (sealed, cool, dark)

**Yield Breakdown:**
- 30 lbs fat trim → 25 lbs rendered grease (main product)
- 3-4 lbs cracklings (crispy bits, bonus food product @ 1 sp/lb)
- 2 lbs waste (unusable tissue)

**Uses:**
- **Cooking:** High smoke point, neutral flavor (better than butter for frying)
- **Waterproofing:** Boots, leather goods, tents (excellent water repellent)
- **Cold protection:** Skin balm prevents frostbite (Northern frontier essential)
- **Medicinal:** Joint pain relief, wound salve (traditional remedy)
- **Light source:** Can be used for lamps/candles (burns clean)
- **Soap making:** Combined with lye (frontier soap)

**Economics:**
- **Critical:** For many bears, fat rendering = 40-50% of total revenue
- Example: 300 lb bear = 30 sp meat + 25 lbs grease @ 5 sp = 125 sp grease
- **Grease value (125 sp) often exceeds meat value (30 sp) 4:1 ratio**

**Storage:** 6-12 months in sealed crocks, cool/dark conditions

### **HIDE / PELT**
**From Skinning Entire Carcass**

**Yield (300 lb Black Bear):**
- **1 hide** (~30-40 lbs raw weight)
- **Quality:** Depends on season, damage, skinning skill

**Value by Quality:**
- **Perfect (trophy mount quality):** 25-35 sp (black bear), up to 120 gp (polar bear)
- **Standard (good condition):** 15-25 sp (black bear)
- **Utility (cuts/tears, usable):** 8-12 sp (salvage value)
- **Damaged (major flaws):** 3-5 sp (scrap leather only)

**Processing:**
1. **Skinning:** Careful removal (avoid knife cuts, minimize holes)
2. **Fleshing:** Remove all fat/meat from skin side (critical - prevents rot)
3. **Salt cure:** Heavy salt coating, 48-72 hours (preserves for transport)
4. **Tanning:** Professional tanning (10-20 sp cost, adds to value)

**Tanning Options:**
- **Brain tanning:** Traditional (soft, supple leather) - 3-5 days work
- **Chemical tanning:** Commercial (durable, weather-resistant) - 10-15 sp professional
- **Fur-on vs leather:** Keep fur for rugs/cloaks OR remove fur for leather goods

**Uses:**
- **Rugs:** Trophy display, floor covering (black bear standard, polar bear luxury)
- **Cloaks:** Winter wear, status symbol (grizzly pelt = adventurer prestige)
- **Armor:** Leather crafting - thick bear hide = AC bonus (cave bear best)
- **Trade goods:** Fur markets (polar bear to southern cities)

**Seasonal Quality:**
- **Winter pelts:** Premium (+20-30%) - thick, full coat
- **Fall pelts:** Baseline - good condition, winter coat growing
- **Summer pelts:** Poor (-30-40%) - thin, shedding
- **Spring pelts:** Worst (-40-50%) - shedding, patchy

**Field Preservation:**
- Must salt cure within 24 hours (prevents bacterial rot)
- If no salt: Dry in shade, air circulation (emergency only, risk of spoilage)
- Cold weather: Can freeze (natural preservation, thaw carefully before tanning)

### **ALCHEMICAL COMPONENTS - GALLBLADDER**
**From Digestive System (Attached to Liver)**

**Yield (All Bears):**
- **1 gallbladder** per bear (contains bile)
- **Size:** Varies by bear size (black bear 2-4 oz, grizzly 6-10 oz)

**Value by Bear Species:**
- **Standard black bear:** 2-4 sp
- **Large brown/grizzly:** 6-15 sp
- **Cave bear:** 8-12 sp (premium medicinal reputation)
- **Polar bear:** 10-20 sp (rare, cold resistance properties)
- **Chultan sun bear:** 20-40 gp (exotic, Chultan medicinal traditions, EXTREME value)

**Harvesting:**
1. Locate gallbladder (attached to underside of liver, small green sac)
2. **CRITICAL:** Do not puncture (bile release ruins it, makes bitter/toxic)
3. Cut carefully around attachment points
4. Tie off bile duct (prevent leakage)
5. Preserve immediately (see below)

**Preservation Options:**
- **Fresh sale:** Sell immediately (1-3 days shelf life max)
- **Dried whole:** Hang to dry in cool/dark place (6-12 months storage)
- **Bile extraction:** Pierce carefully, drain bile into glass vial, seal (12+ months storage)

**Alchemical Uses (D&D 5e):**
- **Digestive remedy:** Potion ingredient (lesser restoration, cure disease)
- **Antitoxin:** Neutralize poison ingredient (detoxification properties)
- **Darkvision potion:** Bear's keen senses (folklore ingredient)
- **Cold resistance:** Polar bear gallbladder specifically (icy habitat adaptation)

**Market:**
- **Buyers:** Alchemists, healers, herbalists, component shops
- **Urban centers:** Waterdeep, Candlekeep, Silverymoon (best prices)
- **Verification:** Use *Detect Magic* to confirm authenticity (fakes common in luxury market)

**Professional Standards:**
- Harvest carefully (don't rupture)
- Know your buyer (establish relationship with alchemist)
- Don't sell fake/damaged gallbladders (ruins reputation)

**References:**
- Master Provisioner's Guide § Wild Herbs & Foraging (medicinal ingredients)
- Candlekeep alchemical suppliers (trusted buyers)

## UNIVERSAL TRUTHS OF BEAR PROCESSING

*These apply to every bear, every time:*

1. **Eight primals, consistent anatomy** - Black bear to grizzly, the structure is the same
2. **Backstrap is king** - Tenderest cut, highest value per pound, never waste it
3. **Fat is fortune** - Bear grease often = 40-50% of total revenue, render it properly
4. **Seasonal variation is extreme** - Fall bear (10% fat) vs spring bear (4% fat) = 150% difference in grease value
5. **Pelts matter seasonally** - Winter coat (+30% value) vs summer coat (-40% value)
6. **Gallbladders are small but valuable** - 1 oz of tissue = 2-40 gp depending on species
7. **Wild game requires thorough cooking** - Trichinosis risk, always 160°F internal minimum
8. **Butcher's choice drives profit** - Know your market: steaks for individuals, roasts for families
9. **Field dressing is critical** - 2-4 hour window before spoilage in warm weather
10. **Trophy value can exceed meat value** - Polar bear pelt (100 gp) often > meat/fat combined
