# ICEWIND DALE POLAR BEAR
## *Ursus maritimus glacialis* - The Arctic Trophy King

**ORIGIN:** Icewind Dale, Reghed Glacier, Sea of Moving Ice
**PRIMARY ROLE:** Premium white pelt (luxury fur trade), extreme cold adaptation
**MARKET POSITION:** Trophy hunting, southern luxury markets, rare arctic specialist

## BREED OVERVIEW & IMPORTANCE

The Icewind Dale polar bear is **not about meat**. You hunt polar bears for one reason: **that white pelt**. 80 to 120 gold pieces for a perfect winter coat, shipped south to Waterdeep nobility. The meat? 60-80 silver. The fat? 80-100 silver. The pelt? 800-1200 silver.

**The math is simple: This is trophy hunting, not provisioning.**

**Why This Breed Matters:**
- **Premium Pelt Market:** White fur = luxury status symbol in southern cities
- **Trophy Value:** Perfect pelt often = 60-70% of total bear value (vs 5-10% for other bears)
- **Extreme Challenge:** CR 2 hunting (requires 3-5 person party, dangerous)
- **Cold Resistance:** Alchemical value (polar bear fat for resist cold potions)
- **Cultural Significance:** Reghed tribal rites, northern prestige
- **Export Economics:** Icewind Dale → Luskan → Waterdeep (long supply chain)

**Market Position:**
- **Luskan fur trade:** Primary market (export hub to southern cities)
- **Waterdeep luxury:** End buyers (noble estates, wealthy merchants)
- **Reghed tribes:** Cultural/subsistence (respect traditions)
- **Adventurer prestige:** Trophy hunting (polar bear cloak = arctic veteran)

**Processing Reality Check:**
- **Extreme Danger:** CR 2 (450 XP), can kill level 1-2 characters in one round
- **Harsh Conditions:** -30°F to -50°F, blizzards, limited daylight
- **Heavy Investment:** 5-7 days hunt, 2-3 days process, 10-20 days transport to Luskan
- **High Transport Costs:** 40-60 gp freight to Waterdeep (200+ miles, harsh terrain)
- **Total Revenue (Perfect Pelt):** 180-250 gp gross, 120-190 gp net after costs
- **Risk:** Party wipe potential, frostbite, arctic storms, spoilage in extreme cold

**Professional Perspective:**
*"I've been to Icewind Dale twice in 132 years. Processed four polar bears. Made 600 gold total. Nearly died three times. Would I do it again? Yes - but only with a veteran party, perfect timing, and solid buyer contracts. This isn't beginner work."*

### Why Arctic Only?

**Biology = Environment:**
- **White coat:** Camouflage for ice/snow hunting (useless in forest)
- **Thick fat layer:** Extreme cold adaptation (10% body fat standard)
- **Marine diet:** Seals, fish, whale carcasses (not available inland)
- **Heat intolerance:** Cannot survive temperate climates (overheats)

**Result:** Polar bears exist ONLY in Icewind Dale region of Western Faerûn

## SEASONAL AVAILABILITY

### Fall Hunting Season (Sept-Nov) **DIFFICULT**

**Early Fall (September):**
- **Condition:** Good (fattening, coastal hunting)
- **Weather:** -10°F to -30°F (tolerable)
- **Hunting:** Accessible (bears active, coast accessible)
- **Pelt quality:** Fair (summer coat still shedding, winter coat 60% grown)

**Mid Fall (October) - OPTIMAL CONDITIONS:**
- **Condition:** Excellent (obese, peak weight)
- **Weather:** -20°F to -40°F (cold but stable)
- **Hunting:** Good (bears active, pre-sea ice formation)
- **Pelt quality:** Good (winter coat 80% grown, thick)
- **Light:** 8-10 hours daylight (enough for hunting)

**Late Fall (November):**
- **Condition:** Excellent (peak fat)
- **Weather:** -30°F to -50°F (extreme cold)
- **Hunting:** Challenging (sea ice forming, bears moving to ice)
- **Pelt quality:** **Excellent (full winter coat, MAXIMUM VALUE)**
- **Light:** 4-6 hours daylight (limited hunting window)

**Professional Timing:**
- **Best compromise:** Early-mid October (good condition, tolerable weather, decent pelt)
- **Best pelt:** Late November (perfect coat, accept extreme conditions)
- **Best safety:** Early September (warmer, better light, accept lower pelt quality)

---

### Winter (Dec-Feb) - **EXTREME CONDITIONS**

**Technically Huntable, But:**
- **Weather:** -40°F to -70°F (life-threatening)
- **Light:** 0-2 hours daylight (polar night)
- **Access:** Sea ice only (bears hunt seals on ice, not on land)
- **Survival:** Extreme risk (frostbite in minutes, hypothermia, storms)
- **Pelt quality:** Perfect (full prime winter coat)

**Professional Standard:** DO NOT HUNT WINTER POLAR BEARS unless you are:
1. Experienced arctic explorer
2. Reghed tribal member (traditional knowledge)
3. Level 5+ adventuring party with cold resistance magic
4. Desperate or insane

**Note:** Unlike temperate bears, polar bears do NOT hibernate. They remain active all winter.

---

### Spring (Mar-May) - **POOR SEASON**

**Post-Winter Condition:**
- **Weight:** Lean (lost 20-30% body weight)
- **Fat yield:** 6-7% (48-56 lbs) - still more than black bear, but reduced
- **Pelt quality:** Shedding (yellow-stained from seal oil, patchy)
- **Mothers with cubs:** Common (born Jan-Feb in snow dens)
- **Hunting:** Illegal in most tribal territories (respect breeding season)

---

### Summer (Jun-Aug) - **OFF-SEASON**

**Summer Condition:**
- **Limited sea ice:** Bears forced to land (less food, losing weight)
- **Pelt quality:** Thin, yellow-stained, low value (30-50% reduction)
- **Behavior:** Opportunistic scavenging (whale carcasses, bird colonies)
- **Survival challenge:** Easier weather (above 0°F), more daylight

**Why Summer Hunting:**
- More accessible terrain (no blizzards, more light)
- Desperation hunting (trapped in Icewind Dale, need income)
- Problem bear removal (threatening Ten-Towns)

**Trade-Off:** Much easier hunt, but pelt value drops 40-60% (50-70 gp vs 100-120 gp)

## PRESERVATION METHODS

### **Arctic Field Preservation (Unique)**

**Advantage:** Below-freezing temperatures = natural freezing

**Process:**
1. Field dress immediately after kill
2. Quarter carcass (4 quarters + pelt separate)
3. Leave outside overnight (-40°F = frozen solid)
4. Transport frozen to Ten-Towns (sled, stays frozen)
5. Process in warm workshop (thaw, then butcher)

**Benefit:** No spoilage risk (frozen = preserved), can take time to transport

**Challenge:** Frozen meat is HARD. Must partially thaw before cutting (3-6 hours warm room).

---

### **Pelt Preservation (CRITICAL - DO NOT SCREW THIS UP)**

**Process:**
1. **Skinning:** Careful removal (one cut ruins 1000 sp value)
2. **Fleshing:** Remove EVERY trace of fat/meat (fat = rancidity = destroyed pelt)
3. **Salt cure:** Heavy salt (10-15 lbs), 72+ hours
4. **Dry:** Cold, dry conditions (arctic air perfect)
5. **Roll and transport:** Careful packaging (don't crease or damage)
6. **Professional tanning:** Hire expert (30-50 gp, non-negotiable for luxury market)

**Common Mistakes (All Ruin Pelt):**
- Incomplete fleshing (fat left on skin = rot)
- Insufficient salt (bacterial growth)
- Folding while damp (mold, creases)
- Amateur tanning attempt (destroys fur quality)

**Professional Standard:** Unless you have Leatherworker's Tools proficiency AND have tanned 5+ pelts, hire a professional. One mistake = lose 100 gp.
