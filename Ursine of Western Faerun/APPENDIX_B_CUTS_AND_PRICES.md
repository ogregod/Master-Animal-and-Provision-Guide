# APPENDIX B: CUTS & PRICES - FOUNDRY VTT MASTER ITEM LIST
## Complete Bear Product Catalog by Breed

**PURPOSE:** This appendix provides exact item counts, weights, and pricing for all bear products across all 8 breeds. Use this as a master reference for creating Foundry VTT items, stocking butcher shops, or determining loot from hunting encounters.

**PRICING BASELINE:** Waterdeep, 1492 DR (standard market)

**WEIGHT UNITS:** Pounds (lbs) for meat/byproducts, ounces (oz) for individual steaks

**CURRENCY:** Silver pieces (sp) and gold pieces (gp) - 10 sp = 1 gp

---

## HOW TO USE THIS APPENDIX

### For Game Masters:
1. **Butcher Shop Inventory:** Use breed tables to stock realistic inventory with exact quantities
2. **Hunting Loot:** Reference the breed the party killed, use that table for field dressing results
3. **Foundry VTT Items:** Create items with exact names, weights, and prices from tables
4. **Seasonal Pricing:** Apply modifiers from Seasonal Pricing section
5. **Regional Markets:** Apply modifiers from Market-Specific Pricing section

### For Players:
1. **Processing Planning:** See exactly what you'll get from each bear breed
2. **Economic Decisions:** Compare fresh vs preserved pricing to plan processing strategy
3. **Market Research:** Know what different cities pay for bear products
4. **Butcher's Choice:** Understand roast vs steak options when commissioning processing

## SECTION 9: SEASONAL PRICING MODIFIERS

**Bear hunting is highly seasonal. Apply these modifiers to base pricing depending on time of year.**

### FALL (Sept-Nov) - **PRIME HUNTING SEASON**

**Bear Condition:** Obese (9-10% body fat, pre-hibernation fattening)

**Pricing Modifiers:**
- **Fresh meat:** +10-15% (peak quality, abundant supply)
- **Bear grease yield:** +40-50% volume (more fat to render)
- **Pelt quality:** +20-30% (winter coat coming in, thick and full)
- **Market availability:** HIGH (most bears processed during this window)

**Best For:**
- Commercial bear hunting (optimal profit)
- Bear grease production (maximum yield)
- Trophy hunting (healthy, well-fed specimens)

**GM Note:** This is when most bears are hunted. Standard pricing assumes fall season.

---

### WINTER (Dec-Feb) - **HIBERNATION (No Hunting)**

**Bear Condition:** Denning (hibernation)

**Ethical/Legal Status:** **OFF-LIMITS** (ethical restrictions, most regions forbid winter den hunting)

**Market Availability:** SCARCE (preserved products from fall hunting)

**Pricing Modifiers (Preserved Products Only):**
- **Smoked bear meat:** +30-50% (scarcity premium, no fresh supply)
- **Bear jerky:** +40-60% (travel rations demand in winter)
- **Bear grease:** +20-30% (cold protection demand, healing salves)
- **Fresh meat:** UNAVAILABLE (no hunting)

**Best For:**
- Selling preserved products from fall hunting
- Bear grease sales (cold protection demand peaks)

---

### SPRING (Mar-May) - **LEAN SEASON**

**Bear Condition:** Very lean (4-5% body fat, post-hibernation)

**Pricing Modifiers:**
- **Fresh meat:** -20-30% (tough, lean, low fat = lower quality)
- **Bear grease yield:** -50-60% volume (minimal fat)
- **Pelt quality:** -30-40% (shedding winter coat, patchy)
- **Market availability:** LOW (few hunters bother with spring bears)

**Why Hunt Spring Bears?**
- Desperation (need meat, no other options)
- Trophy only (pelt/meat value very low)
- Nuisance bears (livestock predation, relocate or kill)

**GM Note:** Experienced hunters avoid spring bears. Not economically viable.

---

### SUMMER (Jun-Aug) - **RECOVERY SEASON**

**Bear Condition:** Moderate (6-7% body fat, rebuilding reserves)

**Pricing Modifiers:**
- **Fresh meat:** -10-15% (acceptable quality but not peak)
- **Bear grease yield:** -20-30% volume (still rebuilding fat)
- **Pelt quality:** -15-20% (summer coat, thinner)
- **Market availability:** MODERATE (some hunting, but not prime)

**Why Hunt Summer Bears?**
- Opportunistic hunting (adventuring parties encounter bears)
- Coastal bears (salmon runs start late summer, bears congregate)
- Moderate profit (better than spring, not as good as fall)

**GM Note:** Summer hunting is acceptable but not optimal. Parties may hunt bears encountered during quests.

## SECTION 11: PRESERVATION PRICING

- **Smoked roasts:** +100-150% over fresh (2-3 sp/lb → 4-6 sp/lb)
- **Smoked ham:** +120-180% (2 sp/lb → 4.4-5.6 sp/lb)
- **Smoked backstrap:** +100-130% (4 sp/lb → 8-9.2 sp/lb)

**Storage:** 2-4 months (cold smoked), 6-12 months (if additionally dried)

**Best Markets:**
- Icewind Dale (winter provisions)
- Frontier settlements (limited fresh meat access)
- Ship provisions (long voyages)

**Labor Cost:** +30-50 sp (curing, smoking, monitoring)

---

### BEAR JERKY

**Process:** 1/4" slicing + 12-24 hour marinade + 24-48 hour drying (or 6-10 hour smoke-dry)

**Pricing Premium:**
- **Bear jerky:** +150-300% over fresh meat weight basis (2 sp/lb fresh → 5-8 sp/lb jerky)
- **Reality:** 10 lbs fresh → 3 lbs jerky (65-70% moisture loss)
- **Effective pricing:** 10 lbs fresh (20 sp) → 3 lbs jerky (15-24 sp) = +250-300% value per input pound

**Storage:** 6-12 months (dry, cool conditions)

**Best Markets:**
- Adventurers (trail rations)
- Caravan guards (lightweight provisions)
- Wilderness expeditions (nutritious, portable)

**Labor Cost:** +20-30 sp (slicing, marinating, drying)
