# MOONSHAE ISLAND ELK
## The Island Specialist - Smaller, Accessible Elk

**Species Classification:** *Cervus elaphus moonshaensis* (Island Cervine, Insular Subspecies)
**Common Names:** Moonshae Elk, Island Elk, Sea Elk, Northlander Stag
**Typical Weight Range:** 500-700 lbs (male), 380-550 lbs (female)
**Challenge Rating:** 1/4 (same as mainl wapiti)
**Rarity:** Common (Moonshae Isles), Absent elsewhere
**Conservation Status:** Stable (island populations, managed hunting)

---

## I. BREED OVERVIEW

### The Island Elk Phenomenon

Moonshae elk represent an **insular subspecies** - smaller cousins of mainland wapiti that evolved isolated on islands. This is a well-documented pattern called **island dwarfing**: large mainland animals become smaller when colonizing islands with limited resources.

**KEY CHARACTERISTICS:**
- **Smaller Than Wapiti:** 500-700 lbs (vs mainland wapiti 750 lbs, **33% smaller**)
- **Larger Than Red Deer:** 500-700 lbs (vs red deer 325 lbs, **50% larger**)
- **Same Danger:** CR 1/4 during rut (despite smaller size, bulls still aggressive)
- **Moderate Yield:** 170-230 lbs retail (vs wapiti 220 lbs, red deer 95 lbs)
- **Accessible Expeditions:** 5-10 days (vs mainland wapiti 5-15 days, slightly easier)
- **Island Shipping Costs:** +20-30 sp per deer (boat to mainland, unique cost)
- **Cultural Role:** Northlander Ffolk = elk-dependent (similar to Reghed/caribou, island survival)
- **Genetic Isolation:** 2,000+ years separated from mainland (developing into distinct subspecies)

**MARKET POSITION:** Moonshae elk occupy the **"accessible expedition elk"** niche:
- **Less Investment:** 5-10 day expeditions (vs mainland wapiti 5-15 days)
- **Smaller Yields:** 170-230 lbs (vs wapiti 220 lbs, 22% less meat)
- **Island Premium:** +20-30% prices (exotic "island elk" marketing, rarity on mainland)
- **Northlander Market:** 60% consumed locally (Moonshae), 40% exported (Waterdeep, Baldur's Gate)

## III. PHYSICAL CHARACTERISTICS

### Size and Build (Island Dwarfing)

**ADULT MALE (Bull):**
- **Live Weight:** 500-700 lbs (avg 600 lbs, **20% smaller than mainland wapiti 750 lbs**)
- **Shoulder Height:** 50-56 inches (vs wapiti 54-60 inches, slightly shorter)
- **Body Length:** 85-100 inches
- **Build:** Stocky, compact (island proportions, shorter legs relative to body)

**ADULT FEMALE (Cow):**
- **Live Weight:** 380-550 lbs (avg 465 lbs, **15% smaller than mainland wapiti cow 550 lbs**)
- **Shoulder Height:** 46-52 inches
- **Body Length:** 80-95 inches

**ISLAND DWARFING EFFECT:**
- **Smaller Body:** 20% lighter than mainland wapiti (limited island resources select for smaller size over 2,000+ years)
- **Shorter Legs:** Proportionally shorter (less need for long-distance migration on islands)
- **Stockier Build:** Compact (cold maritime climate, stocky = better heat retention)

**SIZE COMPARISON:**

|--------|----------|--------------|-----------------|
| Weight | 325 lbs | 600 lbs | 750 lbs |
| Height | 47 inches | 53 inches | 57 inches |
| Retail Yield | 95 lbs | 204 lbs | 220 lbs |
| % of Wapiti | 43% | 80% | 100% |

**POSITIONING:** Moonshae elk = **"80% wapiti"** (same species, island subspecies, 20% smaller in all dimensions).

### Coat and Coloration

**SUMMER COAT (Kythorn-Eleasis):**
- **Body:** Reddish-brown to tan (similar to mainland wapiti)
- **Neck:** Dark brown mane (bulls, slightly less pronounced than mainland)
- **Rump Patch:** Cream to tan (10-14 inches diameter)
- **Shade:** Slightly lighter overall (island populations lighter-coated than mainland, less selection pressure from predators)

**WINTER COAT (Uktar-Mirtul):**
- **Body:** Gray-brown to light tan
- **Neck Mane:** Dark brown to black (shaggy, 60-80 mm long)
- **Rump Patch:** Cream to white
- **Thickness:** 35-45 mm hair (vs mainland wapiti 40-50 mm, slightly thinner due to milder maritime climate)

**IDENTIFYING FEATURES:**
- **Smaller Overall:** Only reliable field identification (same structure as mainland wapiti, just 20% smaller)
- **Lighter Coat:** Subtle (island populations trend lighter, but individual variation overlaps)

### Antler Characteristics

**STRUCTURE (Smaller Wapiti-Style):**

Moonshae elk antlers have **identical structure** to mainland wapiti (brow-bez-trez-fourth-fifth-sixth points), but are **proportionally smaller**.

**TYPICAL MATURE BULL (6-Point, 5-7 years):**
- **Main Beam:** 35-48 inches (vs mainland wapiti 40-55 inches, **12% shorter**)
- **Brow Tine:** 10-15 inches (vs mainland 12-18 inches)
- **Bez Tine:** 8-14 inches (vs mainland 10-16 inches)
- **Inside Spread:** 32-45 inches (vs mainland 36-50 inches)
- **Mass:** 6-12 lbs per antler (vs mainland 8-15 lbs)
- **Total Points:** 12 typical (6×6, same as mainland)

**AGE DEVELOPMENT (Same Pattern, Smaller Scale):**
- **2.5 years:** 4-6 points (raghorn)
- **3.5 years:** 8-10 points
- **4.5 years:** 10-12 points
- **5-7 years:** 12 points (6×6, prime trophy)
- **8+ years:** 12-14 points (7×6 or 6×7, rare)

**ANTLER VALUE:**
- **Good 6×6:** 250-400 sp (vs mainland 300-500 sp, island elk **20% lower value**)
- **Quality 6×6:** 400-650 sp (vs mainland 500-800 sp)
- **Trophy 6×6:** 650-1,000 sp (vs mainland 800-1,200 sp)
- **7×7 (Imperial):** 1,000-1,800 sp (vs mainland 1,500-2,500 sp)

**MARKET PERCEPTION:**
- **"Smaller Wapiti" Discount:** Island elk antlers valued 20-30% below mainland wapiti of same point count (smaller size, lighter mass)
- **"Island Exotic" Premium:** Some buyers pay +10-20% for "island elk" novelty (offsets size discount partially)
- **Net Effect:** Island elk antlers = 10-20% below mainland wapiti value

## IV. SEASONAL BIOLOGY

### Annual Cycle (Maritime Climate Influence)

**SPRING (Ches-Mirtul):**
- **Ches-Tarsakh:** Antler shedding (same timing as mainland)
- **Mirtul:** Calving (1 calf, cows seek coastal meadows OR forest edges)

**SUMMER (Kythorn-Eleasis):**
- **Kythorn-Flamerule:** Coastal grazing (sea cliffs, salt marshes, maritime influence)
- **Flamerule-Eleasis:** Velvet antlers (bulls), calf rearing (cows)

**FALL (Eleint-Uktar):**
- **Eleint:** Pre-rut fattening, BEST HUNTING SEASON
- **Marpenoth:** RUT SEASON (same timing as mainland, CR 1/4 dangerous)
- **Uktar:** Post-rut (bulls exhausted)

**WINTER (Nightal-Alturiak):**
- **Milder Than Mainland:** Maritime climate (10-15°F warmer than mainland High Moor)
- **Less Weight Loss:** 10-15% body weight loss (vs mainland 15-20%, milder winter = less stress)

**CLIMATE ADVANTAGE:** Island elk endure **milder winters** (maritime moderation) = better winter survival, less seasonal weight fluctuation, more consistent meat quality year-round.

---

### Rut Season (Marpenoth, Same Danger)

**RUT BEHAVIOR (Identical to Mainland Wapiti):**
- **Bugling:** Same high-pitched scream-to-grunt (audible 1-2 miles)
- **Wallowing:** Bulls dig wallows, urinate, roll (same behavior)
- **Fighting:** Antler-locking, goring (10-15% injury rate)
- **Aggression:** CR 1/4 (bulls charge within 50-100 yards, DANGEROUS)

**SIZE IRRELEVANT TO DANGER:** Island elk bulls are 20% smaller (600 lbs vs 750 lbs), but still **CR 1/4** (600 lb aggressive elk with antlers = lethal threat to humans).

**MEAT QUALITY IMPACT (Same as Mainland):**
- **Rut Bulls:** EXTREME gamey, 3-5% body fat, tough
- **Cows:** Good quality, 8-10% body fat
- **Professional Standard:** Harvest does OR pre-rut bulls (early Eleint OR early Marpenoth)

## V. CULTURAL SIGNIFICANCE (Ffolk/Northlander)

### Elk in Moonshae Culture

**FFOLK RELIGION (Earthmother Worship):**
- **Sacred Animal:** Elk = Earthmother's gift (provides food, hides, antlers)
- **Sustainable Harvest:** Ffolk harvest quotas (prevent over-hunting, spiritual obligation)
- **Myrloch Vale:** Most sacred elk habitat (druids protect, limited hunting allowed)

**NORTHLANDER SETTLEMENTS:**
- **Primary Meat Source:** 40-60% of protein (island populations elk-dependent)
- **Winter Survival:** Smoked/dried elk = winter stores (similar to Ten-Towns/caribou)
- **Cultural Identity:** "Elk-Lords" (noble families control hunting territories, elk heraldry)

**HUNTING TERRITORIES:**
- **Ffolk (Southern Gwynneth):** Myrloch Vale = restricted (druid permission required, 50-100 gp permits)
- **Northlander (Northern Moray):** Open hunting (clan territories, respect boundaries OR face clan warfare)
- **Neutral Grounds:** Eastern Highlands (Gwynneth), uncontested hunting (free access, no permits)

## VII. FIELD HARVESTING AND PROCESSING

### Yield Calculation (600 lb Moonshae Elk Bull)

**STEP 1: Live to Dressed (65%)**
- Live Weight: 600 lbs
- Dressing %: 65%
- Dressed Weight: 600 × 65% = **390 lbs**

**STEP 2: Dressed to Retail (45%)**
- Dressed Weight: 390 lbs
- Retail %: 45%
- Retail Weight: 390 × 45% = **175-176 lbs**

**FINAL TOTAL YIELD:** 29% (600 lbs live → 176 lbs retail)

**YIELD COMPARISON:**
| Cervine | Live | Dressed | Retail | Island Elk Comparison |
|---------|------|---------|--------|-------------|
| Red Deer | 325 lbs | 211 lbs | 95 lbs | Island elk +85% |
| **Moonshae Elk** | **600 lbs** | **390 lbs** | **176 lbs** | **Baseline (80% of mainland wapiti)** |
| Mainland Wapiti | 750 lbs | 488 lbs | 220 lbs | Mainland +25% |

**POSITIONING:** Island elk yield **80% of mainland wapiti** (176 lbs vs 220 lbs), **185% of red deer** (176 lbs vs 95 lbs).

## VIII. PRICING AND ECONOMICS

### Retail Pricing (Mainland Markets)

**PREMIUM CUTS:**
- **Backstrap Steaks:** 9-13 sp/lb (vs mainland wapiti 8-12 sp/lb, +10-15% "island elk" premium)
- **Tenderloin:** 13-17 sp/lb (vs mainland 12-16 sp/lb)
- **Rib Chops:** 8-11 sp/lb (vs mainland 7-10 sp/lb)

**STANDARD CUTS:**
- **Ham Steaks:** 6-9 sp/lb (vs mainland 5-8 sp/lb, +15-20% premium)
- **Shoulder Roasts:** 5-7 sp/lb (vs mainland 4-6 sp/lb)
- **Ground:** 5-7 sp/lb (vs mainland 4-6 sp/lb)

**PRICING DYNAMICS:**
- **"Island Exotic" Premium:** +10-20% on mainland (Waterdeep, Baldur's Gate buyers pay extra for "Moonshae elk")
- **Smaller Portions:** Offset premium (1.5-2 lb steaks vs mainland 2-3 lb, easier for households to afford whole steaks)
- **Shipping Costs:** Absorbed by butcher (20-30 sp boat fees passed to customer in higher prices)

---

### Whole Bull Value (600 lb Moonshae Elk, 6×6 Trophy)

**MEAT VALUE (176 lbs Retail):**
- **Premium Cuts (28 lbs):** Backstraps (16 lbs @ 11 sp) + Tenderloins (4 lbs @ 15 sp) + Rib Chops (8 lbs @ 10 sp) = 176 + 60 + 80 = **316 sp**
- **Standard Cuts (115 lbs):** Ham Steaks (72 lbs @ 7 sp) + Shoulder Roasts (43 lbs @ 6 sp) = 504 + 258 = **762 sp**
- **Economy/Ground (33 lbs):** 33 lbs @ 5 sp = **165 sp**
- **SUBTOTAL MEAT:** 1,243 sp = **124.3 gp**

**BYPRODUCT VALUE:**
- **Hide:** 6-9 gp (elk-quality leather, 20-30 sq ft)
- **Antlers (6×6, island elk):** 400 sp = 40 gp (20% below mainland 6×6)
- **Heart/Liver:** 25 sp
- **SUBTOTAL BYPRODUCTS:** 46.25-49.25 gp

**TOTAL GROSS VALUE:** 124.3 + 46.25-49.25 = **170.55-173.55 gp**

**COSTS:**
- **Expedition (Per Hunter, 3-Hunter Group):** 117-150 gp
- **Processing:** 4-5.6 gp
- **Boat Cargo Fee (Meat):** 15-25 gp (already included in expedition cost above, but separate line item)
- **Packaging:** 2-3 gp
- **TOTAL COSTS (Solo Economics):** 138-184 gp

**NET VALUE (Group Hunt, Costs Divided):**
- Gross: 170.55-173.55 gp
- Costs (Expedition ÷ 3 + Processing + Individual Fees): 39-50 gp + 4-5.6 gp + 5-8 gp = 48-64 gp
- **NET: 106.55-125.55 gp per bull**

**ROI:**
- **Investment:** 117-150 gp per hunter
- **Return:** 106.55-125.55 gp (if successful)
- **ROI:** -9% to +7% (LOW, break-even OR slight loss on single elk)

**VOLUME REQUIRED FOR PROFIT:**
- **2 Elk per Group:** 213-251 gp gross - 117-150 gp investment = 63-134 gp profit
- **Break-Even:** Minimum 1.5 elk per hunter to profit (vs mainland wapiti profitable at 1 elk)

**ECONOMIC REALITY:** Island elk hunting is **marginally profitable** (boat costs eat into margins). Most hunters pursue for:
- **Trophy Prestige:** "Moonshae elk" = exotic (antlers worth +10-20% marketing value)
- **Combination Hunt:** Hunt elk + trade/tourism on islands (multi-purpose trip)
- **Local Consumption:** Moonshae residents (no boat costs, profitable)
